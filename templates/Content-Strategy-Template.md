## Content Audit Framework

Conduct content audits quarterly or bi-annually to assess performance and identify opportunities.

---

### Content Inventory

**Step 1: Inventory all content**
- Compile list of all published content
- Track: URL, title, publish date, last updated, format, pillar, keywords

**Step 2: Collect performance data**
- Traffic (sessions, pageviews)
- Engagement (time on page, bounce rate, scroll depth)
- Conversions (leads, sales, signups)
- Rankings (keyword positions)
- Social shares and engagement

**Step 3: Assess quality**
- Accuracy and currency
- Brand alignment
- SEO optimization
- User experience

---

### Content Audit Template

| URL | Title | Publish Date | Last Updated | Format | Pillar | Primary Keyword | Traffic (30d) | Conversions | Ranking | Quality Score | Action | Priority | Notes |
|-----|-------|--------------|--------------|--------|--------|----------------|--------------|-------------|---------|---------------|--------|----------|-------|
| | | | | | | | | | | 1-5 | Keep/Update/Consolidate/Remove | High/Med/Low | |

---

### Content Classification

**Keep (Green)**:
- High traffic and engagement
- Strong conversions
- Good rankings
- Current and accurate
- **Action**: Minimal—monitor performance

**Update (Yellow)**:
- Decent performance but declining
- Outdated information
- SEO opportunity
- Could be improved
- **Action**: Refresh and optimize

**Consolidate (Orange)**:
- Multiple articles on similar topics
- Thin or overlapping content
- Cannibalization issues
- **Action**: Merge into comprehensive piece

**Remove (Red)**:
- No traffic or engagement
- Outdated and irrelevant
- Off-brand
- Duplicate content
- **Action**: 301 redirect or delete
